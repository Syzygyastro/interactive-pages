What is RIIO?

The framework Ofgem uses to set what monopoly network companies are allowed to earn — and what they have to deliver in return.

RIIO stands for Revenue = Incentives + Innovation + Outputs. Each price-control period (typically 5 years for ED2/ED3) Ofgem agrees with each DNO a totex allowance, a set of outputs the DNO must deliver, and a package of incentives (financial and reputational) that flex revenue up or down based on performance. The current control for distribution is RIIO-ED2; the next is RIIO-ED3, which Ofgem is designing now.

Why this matters to Grid Agents Every DNO connection process, study workflow, system design choice, and innovation budget the team sees referenced in the field is shaped by the active RIIO control. ED3 is the window where the regulatory framing for AI-driven planning, automated curtailment, and anticipatory investment is actually being written.
