What is RIIO?

The framework Ofgem uses to set what monopoly network companies are allowed to earn — and what they have to deliver in return.

RIIO stands for Revenue = Incentives + Innovation + Outputs. Each price-control period (5 years for the T2/T3/ED2/ED3 cycle) Ofgem agrees with each licensee a totex allowance, a set of outputs the licensee must deliver, and a package of incentives (financial and reputational) that flex revenue up or down based on performance. There are four live controls today:

  • RIIO-T2 (2021 – 2026) — just closed; final reconciliation in flight.
  • RIIO-T3 (2026 – 2031) — just started 1 April 2026.
  • RIIO-ED2 (2023 – 2028) — mid-control; ~38 % through delivery.
  • RIIO-ED3 (2028 – 2033) — methodology stage; SSMD due 2026.
Why this matters to Grid Agents Every connection process, study workflow, and innovation budget the team meets in the field is shaped by the active RIIO control on either side of the meter. T3 just started and ED3 is being written this year — this is the window where regulatory framing for AI-driven planning, automated curtailment, and anticipatory investment is actually decided.

RIIO-T2 vs RIIO-T3 — transmission price controls

T2 just closed (March 2026); T3 is now live. Ofgem's Final Determinations for T3 land the largest single allowance step-change in network history, driven by ASTI delivery flowing into baseline, the SSEP/CSNP pipeline, and post-CMP434/435 connection-reform investment.

ASTI — the £20 bn sidecar

Accelerated Strategic Transmission Investment is the bespoke regulatory regime Ofgem ran in parallel with T2 to fast-track 26 large network projects (HVDC links, onshore reinforcement) needed by 2030.

  • Fixed Output Delivery Incentive (FOI) — financial reward for hitting in-service dates and penalties for slippage.
  • Fixed Cost Incentive (FCI) — fixed cap on costs with limited reopeners; TO bears overrun risk above the cap.
  • Streamlined consenting pathway alongside DESNZ and Planning Inspectorate.
  • ASTI delivery rolls into T3 baseline allowance for ongoing operation and incentive treatment.

RIIO-ED2 vs RIIO-ED3 — distribution price controls

ED2 is the live control for the 14 DNO licences. ED3 methodology is being written now — this is the window to influence connection, anticipatory-investment and AI-readiness obligations.

GB network licensees

3 transmission owners + the system operator (NESO) at the top; 14 distribution licences in 6 ownership groups underneath.

Outputs & incentives — how networks are held to account

Each control is built from a stack of obligations, deliverables, and incentives. These are the levers that change network behaviour week-to-week.

Where Grid Agents lands ODI-Fs that touch connection customers and queue management — TTC, ICE, customer-satisfaction surveys, complaints metrics, queue-progression milestones — are exactly the metrics an AI-assisted connection workflow can move.
